Rabbits

Rabbits are gentle, social pets that thrive with proper care and companionship. Their playful nature and soft fur make them wonderful additions to a loving home.

How To Care For Your Rabbit

1. Provide a spacious, clean enclosure with soft bedding.

2. Feed a fiber-rich diet, including hay, vegetables, and pellets.

3. Offer chew toys to maintain healthy teeth.

4. Ensure gentle handling and social interactions.

5. Schedule regular veterinary check-ups to prevent illnesses.

How To Identify If a Rabbit Has a Disease

Signs of illness in rabbits include:

1. Loss of appetite or weight changes.

2. Teeth grinding or excessive drooling.

3. Unusual posture or difficulty moving.

4. Watery eyes, nasal discharge, or difficulty breathing.

5. Diarrhea or constipation.

Rabbits hide signs of illness, If you notice any of these symptoms, consult a veterinarian immediately. Early detection can prevent complications and ensure timely treatment.
